# Quick Start

## 1. Load CHAKRABOOST

Insert CHAKRABOOST as an audio effect on any track in your DAW. Send audio through it — drums, synths, vocals, full mixes.

## 2. All 8 Bands Are Active

Out of the box, all 8 chakra bands are active with default settings:

| Parameter | Default |
|-----------|---------|
| **Phaser Depth** | 0.5 |
| **Phaser Feedback** | 0.5 |
| **Phaser Mix** | 0.5 |
| **LFO Rate** | 0.5 Hz |
| **Compressor Threshold** | -20 dB |
| **Compressor Ratio** | 4:1 |
| **Compressor Mix** | 50% |

You should hear subtle phase modulation across the full spectrum immediately.

## 3. Shape the Phaser

Adjust the phaser on individual bands to hear targeted phase movement:

| Parameter | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Depth** | Controls how far the phaser's notch frequencies sweep — higher values produce more dramatic frequency cancellation |
| **Rate** | Speed of the LFO driving the phaser sweep |
| **Feedback** | Increases resonance at the notch frequencies for a sharper, more vocal phaser tone |
| **Mix** | Blend between dry signal and phased signal per band |

Try increasing **Depth** and **Feedback** on the Heart (250-500 Hz) and Throat (500-1000 Hz) bands for pronounced midrange phasing while leaving the Root and Transcendence bands subtle.

## 4. Enable Compression

Each band has its own adaptive parallel compressor. Adjust the **Threshold** to catch the dynamics of that specific frequency range. The compressor uses real-time crest-factor analysis, so it adapts automatically to the character of the material in each band.

Lower the **Compressor Mix** for transparent glue, or push it higher for aggressive density in specific bands.

## 5. Enable Spatial Panning

Activate spatial panning on any band to place it in the stereo or surround field. Each band can orbit independently using its own LFO-driven trajectory.

- In **Stereo** mode, bands pan left-right with adjustable width
- In **Quad**, **5.1**, or **7.1** mode, bands follow orbital paths across the full speaker layout

Try sending the Root band to center, the Heart band on a slow stereo orbit, and the Crown band on a fast wide orbit for frequency-dependent spatial movement.

## 6. Try the Neural LFO

Switch any band's LFO waveform from a standard shape to **Neural LFO** mode. This engages a proprietary modulation system that produces organic, evolving modulation curves — the phaser sweep will feel alive and non-repetitive compared to standard sine or triangle waveforms.

## 7. Solo and Compare

Use the **Solo** button on any band to isolate it and hear exactly what the phaser, compressor, and spatial panner are doing to that frequency range. Use **Mute** to remove specific bands from the output. Use **Modulation Lock** to freeze a band's current LFO state while you adjust other bands.

<Tip>
CHAKRABOOST works particularly well on full mixes and submixes where you want frequency-specific spatial movement. Try it on a drum bus with subtle phasing on the high bands and compression on the lows, or on a pad with Neural LFO phasing and slow surround orbits across all bands.
</Tip>
