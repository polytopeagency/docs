# Tips & Recipes

## Processing Recipes

### Subtle Stereo Widening
- **Phaser:** Low depth (0.1–0.2), moderate mix (30–40%)
- **LFO:** Sine waveform, slow rate (0.2–0.5 Hz)
- **Spatial:** Wide pan depth (0.5–0.8) across bands — pan low bands slightly left, high bands slightly right
- **Compression:** Off (comp mix 0%)

Gentle per-band phase movement with opposing pan positions creates a wide, natural stereo image without artifacts. Works well on master bus or submixes.

### Aggressive Multiband Phasing
- **Phaser:** High depth (0.7–1.0), high feedback (0.7–0.9), full mix (100%)
- **LFO:** Different rates per band — try ascending rates from Root (0.3 Hz) to Transcendence (8 Hz)
- **Spatial:** Narrow pan depth (0.0–0.1)
- **Compression:** Gentle threshold (−12 dB), low ratio (2:1) to tame resonant peaks

Each band phases at its own rate, creating a dense, evolving texture. The ascending rate pattern produces a barber-pole effect where higher frequencies shimmer faster than lower ones.

### Parallel Compression Mastering
- **Phaser:** Off (depth 0, mix 0%)
- **LFO:** Any (ignored when phaser is off)
- **Spatial:** Leave at center (pan 0.0, pan depth 0.0)
- **Compression:** Gentle thresholds (−18 to −12 dB), moderate ratios (3:1–4:1), 50% comp mix across all bands
- **Utility:** Fine-tune per-band gain to shape the tonal balance

Uses CHAKRABOOST purely as an 8-band parallel compressor. The 50% comp mix blends compressed and dry signals per band, adding density and glue without destroying transients. Adjust per-band thresholds to compress only bands that need control.

### Surround Spatial Orbits
- **Speaker Layout:** 5.1 or 7.1
- **Spatial:** Set pan depth (0.5–1.0) and pan depth Y (0.5–1.0) for circular orbits in the surround field
- **Orbit Angle:** Vary per band — try spacing bands evenly (0°, 45°, 90°, 135°, 180°, 225°, 270°, 315°)
- **LFO:** Moderate rates (0.5–2.0 Hz) — different rates per band for complex spatial patterns
- **Phaser:** Optional — low depth for subtle movement, or off for pure spatial processing

Each band traces its own orbit through the surround field. Staggering orbit angles ensures bands arrive at different speaker positions at any given moment, creating immersive frequency-dependent spatial motion.

### Neural Modulation Texture
- **LFO Mode:** Neural (on all bands or selected bands)
- **Neural Rhythm:** Start at 0.5, explore extremes for different rhythmic characters
- **Neural Articulation:** Varies the shape of the modulation curve — low values are smooth, high values are more angular
- **Neural Temperature:** Controls variation — low temperature produces predictable patterns, high temperature introduces organic unpredictability
- **Phaser:** Moderate depth (0.4–0.6), moderate feedback (0.3–0.5)

The proprietary modulation system produces evolving, non-repeating modulation curves that escape the mechanical feel of standard LFO waveforms. Try setting different rhythm and articulation values per band for maximum textural complexity.

## Workflow Tips

### Use Solo to tune individual bands
Solo each band while adjusting its phaser, compression, and spatial settings. This lets you hear exactly what each chakra range is contributing before hearing the full mix.

### Automate Dry/Wet for effect sends
Instead of using CHAKRABOOST at 100% wet on an insert, try automating the Dry/Wet parameter. Sweep from 0% to 100% during a build-up for a gradual introduction of the effect.

### Match compression to band content
Lower bands (Root, Sacral) often benefit from slower attack and release times to preserve bass punch. Higher bands (Crown, Transcendence) can use faster settings to control sibilance and transients without audible pumping.

### Bypass as A/B reference
Use the global Bypass parameter to quickly compare processed and unprocessed signal. The linear-phase crossover ensures no coloration when bypassed — what you hear is purely the effect of the phasing, compression, and spatial processing.

## Gotchas

### Latency
The 255-tap FIR crossover introduces approximately **127 samples of latency** (~2.9ms at 44.1kHz). This is reported to the host DAW for automatic delay compensation. If you experience timing issues, verify your DAW's plugin delay compensation is enabled.

### Mono summing behavior
The crossover filter bank operates in **mono per channel** — left and right channels are split independently, then processed through per-band effects, then recombined to stereo (or surround). This means the stereo image is preserved through the crossover but each band's phaser operates on the summed mono signal of that band before stereo recovery via spatial panning.

### Feedback limit
Phaser feedback is capped at **0.95** to prevent runaway self-oscillation. Even at 0.95, resonant peaks can be aggressive — use per-band compression (or reduce feedback) if you hear excessive ringing.

### Neural LFO parameters without Neural mode
The Neural Rhythm, Neural Articulation, Neural Depth, and Neural Temperature parameters only have effect when the LFO Mode is set to Neural. In all other LFO modes (Sine, Triangle, Saw, etc.), these parameters are ignored.
